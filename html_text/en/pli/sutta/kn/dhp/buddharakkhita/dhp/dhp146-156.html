Old Age

SC 146When this world is ever ablaze, why this laughter, why this jubilation? Shrouded in darkness, will you not see the light?

SC 147Behold this body—a painted image, a mass of heaped up sores, infirm, full of hankering—of which nothing is lasting or stable!

SC 148Fully worn out is this body, a nest of disease, and fragile. This foul mass breaks up, for death is the end of life.

SC 149These dove-colored bones are like gourds that lie scattered about in autumn. Having seen them, how can one seek delight?

SC 150This city (body) is built of bones, plastered with flesh and blood; within are decay and death, pride and jealousy.

SC 151Even gorgeous royal chariots wear out, and indeed this body too wears out. But the Dhamma of the Good does not age; thus the Good make it known to the good.

SC 152The man of little learning grows old like a bull. He grows only in bulk, but, his wisdom does not grow.

SC 153Through many a birth in samsara have I wandered in vain, seeking the builder of this house (of life). Repeated birth is indeed suffering!

SC 154O house-builder, you are seen! You will not build this house again. For your rafters are broken and your ridgepole shattered. My mind has reached the Unconditioned; I have attained the destruction of craving.

SC 155Those who in youth have not led the holy life, or have failed to acquire wealth, languish like old cranes in the pond without fish.

SC 156Those who in youth have not lead the holy life, or have failed to acquire wealth, lie sighing over the past, like worn out arrows (shot from) a bow.

Ācāriya Buddharakkhita, The Dhammapada: The Buddha’s Path of Wisdom, Buddhist Publication Society, 1985. (Revised, 1996).
