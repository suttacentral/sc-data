371. {374.} Paṭṭipupphiya

Walt 3241 When the Sambuddha, the Great Sage,
Padumuttara passed away,
all the people came together;
they are carrying off his corpse. Verse 1

Walt 3242 When the corpse was being removed,
when the drums were being sounded,
happy, with pleasure in my heart,
I offered a red lodh flower. Verse 2

Walt 3243 In the hundred thousand aeons
since I did that flower-pūjā,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
the fruit of worshipping relics. Verse 3

Walt 3244 Being in Best Buddha’s presence
was a very good thing for me.
The three knowledges are attained;
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 4

Walt 3245 My defilements are Walt 3245 My defilements are now burnt up;
all new existence is destroyed.
Like elephants with broken chains,
I am living without constraint. Verse 5

Walt 3246 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 6

Thus indeed Venerable Paṭṭipupphiya Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Paṭṭipupphiya Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
