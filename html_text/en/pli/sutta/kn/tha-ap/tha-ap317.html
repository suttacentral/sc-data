315. Pupphâsaniya

Walt 2787 The golden-colored Sambuddha,
just like the hundred-raying sun,
was traveling near by to me,
Siddhattha, the Unconquered One. Verse 1

Walt 2788 Going out to meet the Buddha,
having him enter my ashram,
I gave him a seat of flowers,
with a mind that was very clear. Verse 2

Walt 2789 Pressing both my hands together,
at that time I was overjoyed.
Pleasing my heart in the Buddha,
I turned that karma to my use. Verse 3

Walt 2790 I’m pure in the dispensation
because of all that wholesomeness,
the merit made by me in the
Unconquered One, the Self-Become. Verse 4

Walt 2791 In the ninety-four aeons since
I gave that floral seat back then,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
the fruit of giving floral seats. Verse 5

Walt 2792 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 6

Thus indeed Venerable Pupphâsaniya Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Pupphâsaniya Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.

Whitman College, 2017
