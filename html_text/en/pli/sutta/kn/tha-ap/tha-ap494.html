489. {492.} Paŋsukūlapūjaka

Walt 5256 In the Himalayan region,
there’s a mountain named Udaka.
There I saw the Buddha’s rag-robe,
stuck up in the top of a tree. Verse 1

Walt 5257 Plucking three kiṅkhani flowers,
that were growing there at that time,
happy, and with a happy heart,
I offered them to that rag-robe. Verse 2

Walt 5258 Due to that karma done very well,
with intention and firm resolve,
discarding my human body,
I went to Tāvatiṃsa then. Verse 3

Walt 5259 In the ninety-one aeons since
I did pūjā to that Buddha,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
that’s the fruit of Buddha-pūjā. Verse 4

Walt 5260 My defilements are now burnt up;
all new existence is destroyed.
Like elephants with broken chains,
I am living without constraint. Verse 5

Walt 5261 Being in Best Buddha’s presence
was a very good thing for me.
The three knowledges are attained;
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 6

Walt 5262 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 7

Thus indeed Venerable Paŋsukūlapūjaka Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Paŋsukūlapūjaka Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
