432. {435.} Ucchukaṇḍika

Walt 4673 In the city, Bandhumatī,
I was a gate-keeper back then.
I saw the Buddha, Stainless One,
who was Master of Everything. Verse 1

Walt 4674 Happy, with pleasure in Walt 4674 Happy, with pleasure in my heart,
bringing a stalk of sugarcane,
I gave it to him, the Best Buddha,
the Great Sage known as Vipassi. Verse 2

Walt 4675 In the ninety-one aeons since
I gave the Buddha sugar-cane,
I’ve come to know no bad rebirth:
the fruit of a sugar-cane stalk. Verse 3

Walt 4676 My defilements are Walt 4676 My defilements are now burnt up;
all new existence is destroyed.
Like elephants with broken chains,
I am living without constraint. Verse 4

Walt 4677 Being in Best Buddha’s presence
was a very good thing for me.
The three knowledges are attained;
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 5

Walt 4678 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 6

Thus indeed Venerable Ucchukaṇḍika Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Ucchukaṇḍika Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
