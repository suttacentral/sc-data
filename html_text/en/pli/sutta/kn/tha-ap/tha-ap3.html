attaining wisdom’s perfection
they always gather around you. Verse 172

Walt 312 Such indeed are they, Great Hero,
your students, who are so well-trained,
hard to approach, with mighty powers,
they always gather around you. Verse 173

Walt 313 Surrounded by Walt 313 Surrounded by all those students
ascetics who have been taught well,
like a lion which is not frightened
you shine just like the king of stars. Verse 174

Walt 314 Having been planted in the earth,
hardwood trees grow up strong and tall.
They attain their full abundance
and in season display their fruit. Verse 175

Walt 315 O Śākyas’ Son, O Great Famed One,
you’re analogous to the earth;
being fixed in your great teaching,
they like the trees grow deathless fruit. Verse 176

Walt 316 The Indus, and the Sarasvatī
are rivers, like the Candabhāgā,
the Ganges and the Yamuna
the Sarabhu and the Mahī too. Verse 177

Walt 317 When those rivers Walt 317 When those rivers finish flowing
the great ocean accepts them all.
Abandoning their former names,
they’re all known as “the great ocean”. Verse 178

Walt 318 Likewise these people, of four castes,
who’ve gone forth into your presence,
abandoning their former names
are all known as “the Buddha’s sons”. Verse 179

Walt 319 Just as the moon which is unblemished
going across the space in the sky
casting its light upon the world
outshines the entire mass of stars, Verse 180

Walt 320 so likewise you, O Great Hero,
surrounded by the gods and men,
going across the Buddha-field
are shining brightly all the time. Verse 181

Walt 321 Waves which Walt 321 Waves which first arise from the depths
go no further than the seashore;
when they do come onto the shore,
they are crushed to bits and scattered. Verse 182

Walt 322 Just so the world’s heretics
with their varied views and big crowds
wishing to possess the Teaching
never go further than the Sage. Verse 183

Walt 323 If they Walt 323 If they try attaining to that
through debating, O Eyeful One,
having come into your presence
they get thoroughly crushed by you. Verse 184

Walt 324 Just as many white lotuses
and mandālaka blooms, water-born,
do get besmeared by the water
and also by the mud and clay, Verse 185

Walt 325 so too indeed many creatures
who’re born and grow up in the world
are pained by their lust and anger
like the white lotus in the mud. Verse 186

Walt 326 Just as a pink lotus, water-born,
growing up in the water’s midst
is not besmeared by the water
but rather that lotus is clean, Verse 187

Walt 327 so too are you, O Great Hero,
though born within the world, Great Sage.
You are not besmeared by the world,
like the pink lotus by water. Verse 188

Walt 328 Likewise, many lotus flowers
blossom in the month of April
but do not last beyond that month;
that is the time for blossoming. Verse 189

Walt 329 So too are you, O Śākyas’ Son
blooming in your liberation.
The dispensation’s not surpassed
like the water-born lotuses. Verse 190

Walt 330 The king of Walt 330 The king of sal trees all in bloom
exudes a heavenly perfume.
Surrounded by other sal trees
the king of sal trees is lovely. Verse 191

Walt 331 So too are you, O Great Hero,
blooming with a Buddha’s wisdom.
Circled by the monks’ Assembly,
like the sal-king you are lovely. Verse 192

Walt 332 Just as the Himalayan stone’s
medicine for living beings
and the lair of the lesser gods,
and nāgas and asurās too, Verse 193

Walt 333 so too are you, O Great Hero,
medicine for living beings;
you’ve mastered the three knowledges,
special knowledges, great powers. Verse 194

Walt 334 They are admonished, Great Hero,
by you, but with great compassion.
Delighting in love of Dhamma
they dwell in your dispensation. Verse 195

Walt 335 Likewise a lion, king of beasts,
going about how he wishes,
surveying the four directions
then growls three times his mighty roar. Verse 196

Walt 336 All the beasts are very frightened
because of that lion’s growling.
Thus just one beast, of noble birth
always frightens all of the rest. Verse 197

Walt 337 Because of your growl, Great Hero,
the earth herself begins to quake.
Those fit for wisdom realize it,
scaring the partisans of Death. Verse 198

Walt 338 The heretics are all afraid
of your voice, O Sage so Great.
That flock of crows is in a fluster
like the beasts with the lion-king. Verse 199

Walt 339 Those with followers in the world
are known by the title “teachers”.
They teach to their community
doctrines passed down by tradition. Verse 200

Walt 340 Not so do you, O Great Hero
preach your Teaching to living beings.
Understanding the truths yourself
you preach all of Awakening. Verse 201

Walt 341 Grasping desires and deep fantasies,
strengths and weaknesses of senses,
discerning who’s able, who’s not,
you thunder forth like a great cloud. Verse 202

Walt 342 Right to the universe’s edge,
seated groups of followers are
thinking through their varied doctrines,
trying to resolve all their doubts. Verse 203

Walt 343 Reading the minds of everyone,
skilled in analogies, O Sage,
discoursing on single questions
you resolve living beings’ doubts. Verse 204

Walt 344 In this world the earth is filled with
people like those I’ve referred to.
All of them, hands reverently pressed,
should sing the World-Leader’s praises. Verse 205

Walt 345 Singing praises for an entire aeon,
speaking of diverse qualities
they never could be fully measured;
the Thus-Gone-One has no measure. Verse 206

Walt 346 Thus singing the Victor’s praises
with all the power that they have,
speaking for ten million aeons
this and that would remain unsaid. Verse 207

Walt 347 If any being, god or man,
even if he’s very well-trained
tries to draw the full ocean’s water
he would certainly come to grief. Verse 208

Walt 348 Walt 348 Now fixed in your dispensation,
O Śākyas’ Son, O Great Famed One
having reached Wisdom’s Perfection
I’m living without defilements. Verse 209

Walt 349 Defeating rival heretics
I further the dispensation.
Today I’m the Dhamma’s general
in the Buddha’s dispensation. Verse 210

Walt 350 Karma done immeasurable
aeons hence showed me its fruit here:
well-liberated, arrow-quick,
I have destroyed my defilements. Verse 211

Walt 351 Whatever man who on his head
would carry a load, every day,
he’d be oppressed due to that load,
and that burden would be heavy. Verse 212

Walt 352 I transmigrated through lifetimes
being burnt up by the three fires,
weighed down by the burden of being
as though I were lifting mountains. Verse 213

Walt 353 My burden Walt 353 My burden now has been laid down
and I’ve destroyed re-becoming.
I’ve done all things that should be done
in the Buddha’s dispensation. Verse 214

Walt 354 Through this entire Buddha-field,
except the Śākyan Bull himself,
I’m supreme in terms of wisdom;
there is no one to rival me. Verse 215

Walt 355 So well-trained in meditation
excelling in the superpowers,
today my only desire is to
create a thousand magically. Verse 216

Walt 356 Of me who dwelt there by and by
the Great Sage was the great Teacher.
He told me the dispensation;
cessation happened lying down. Verse 217

Walt 357 My divine eye is purified
and I’m skilled in concentration.
Proper exertion is applied;
I love wisdom’s parts as focus. Verse 218

Walt 358 Everything is done by me
which followers ought to attain.
Except the Leader of the World
there is no one to rival me. Verse 219

Walt 359 Skilled in the attainments and discipline,
through altered states I got liberated fast.
Fond of wisdom’s parts as focus
I’ve excelled in the followers’ virtues. Verse 220

I’m honored by the Best of Men.
My mind is always filled with faith
in fellow religious students. Verse 221

Walt 361 Like a snake whose poison’s destroyed,
like a bull whose horns are broken,
freed of my pride and arrogance
I approach with great reverence. Verse 222

Walt 362 If my wisdom were a beautiful girl
she’d hook up with the rulers of earth.
This is the fruit of my having praised the
knowledge of Anomadassi Buddha. Verse 223

Walt 363I help keep rolling perfectly

that’s the fruit of praising knowledge. Verse 224

Walt 364 May I not ever, anywhere,
meet one whose thoughts are less than pure,
who’s lazy or lacks energy,
is unlearned or immoral. Verse 225

Walt 365 Let only one who is learned,
wise, well-fixed in moral precepts
and settled into mental calm
come face-to-face in front of me. Verse 226

Walt 366 I’m saying this to you, O monks,
gathered together begging here:
always be happy, with slight wants,
meditators, trance-lovers. Verse 227

Walt 367 That one whom I saw first of all

He’s my teacher, he’s the hero,
the follower named Assaji. Verse 228

Walt 368 It’s on account of him that I
today am Dhamma’s general.
In every place, having excelled,
I’m living without defilements. Verse 229

Walt 369 I bow my head in reverence
to whatever region he’s in,
that one who was my own teacher,
the follower named Assaji. Verse 230

Gotama, Bull of the Śākyas,
seated in the monks’ Assembly
placed me in the foremost place then. Verse 231
