118. Maggasaññaka

Walt 1742 Padumuttara Buddha’s Walt 1742 Padumuttara Buddha’s own
followers, who were forest-monks,
were lost in a giant forest,
wandering there like they were blind. Verse 1

Walt 1743 Calling to mind the Sambuddha,
Padumuttara, the Leader,
and they who were that Sage’s sons,
lost in the great forest by then, Verse 2

Walt 1744 descending from Walt 1744 descending from my residence
I went to where the monks were then
and having shown the road to them
I also gave them food to eat. Verse 3

Walt 1745 Due to that deed for the Biped-Lord,
the World’s Best One, the Bull of Men,
being only seven years old,
I attained my arahantship. Verse 4

Walt 1746 In the five hundredth aeon hence,
there were twelve wheel-turning monarchs
known by the name of Sacakkhu
possessors of the seven gems. Verse 5

Walt 1747 The four analytical modes,
and these eight deliverances,
six special knowledges mastered,
I have done what the Buddha taught! Verse 6

Thus indeed Venerable Maggasaññaka Thera spoke these verses.

The legend of Maggasaññaka Thera is finished.

Translated by Jonathan S. Walters.
