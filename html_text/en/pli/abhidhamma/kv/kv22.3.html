22.3 Of Imperturbable (Fourth Jhāna) Consciousness

Controverted Point: That the Arahant completes existence in imperturbable absorption (āneñje).

Theravādin: PTS cs 22.3.1But does he not complete existence with ordinary (or normal) consciousness? You agree. How, then, do you reconcile this with your proposition?

PTS cs 22.3.2You are implying that he passes away with an ethically inoperative consciousness. Is it not rather with a consciousness that is pure “result”? PTS cs 22.3.3 Whereas according to you he passes away with a consciousness that is unmoral and purely inoperative, I suggest that it is with a consciousness that is unmoral and purely resultant.

PTS cs 22.3.4And did not the Exalted One emerge from Fourth Jhāna before he passed utterly away immediately after?
