14. Hatred

Rock 1Hatred towards those who do no evil and who do not hate, this is the sign Rock 1Hatred towards those who do no evil and who do not hate, this is the sign (lit. garment) of the sinner in this world and in the other.

Rock 2After having brought misery on himself, he would afterwards bring trouble to others, like unto the hunting hawk, Rock 2After having brought misery on himself, he would afterwards bring trouble to others, like unto the hunting hawk, (?) who, captive himself, does injury to others.

Rock 3He who smites will be smitten; he who shows rancour will find rancour; so likewise from reviling comes reviling, and to him who is angered comes anger.

Rock 4Those foolish śramanas who know not the holy law, though this life be brief, in the foolishness of their hearts they give themselves to wrangling.

Rock 5“This is the best Rock 5“This is the best (man),” they think, being thus at variance with the unanimous opinion: “Why does the clergy choose this man? he is without strength and without mind.”

Rock 6If a bone is broken, Rock 6If a bone is broken, (they advise) killing, carrying off horses, cattle, and riches, subduing kingdoms, and then afterwards to become friends again.

Rock 7-8But the wise man, who knows what is (right), says: “Why do you not learn this law, which teaches the real way to live?” (lit. field of activity). You who have not the conduct of sages, watch carefully over the words of your mouth; guard those of your neighbours who do not know (the danger they may run); they who know Rock 7-8But the wise man, who knows what is (right), says: “Why do you not learn this law, which teaches the real way to live?” (lit. field of activity). You who have not the conduct of sages, watch carefully over the words of your mouth; guard those of your neighbours who do not know (the danger they may run); they who know (this law), speak soft-toned pleasing words.”

Rock 9“He abused me, he reviled me, he beat me, he subdued me;” he who keeps this in his mind, and who feels resentment, will find no peace.

Rock 10“He abused me, he reviled me, he beat me, he subdued me;” he who keeps not this in his mind, and is not resentful, will find peace.

Rock 11He who shows hatred to those who hate will never be at peace; he who is patient with those who hate will find peace; this is the spirit of religion.

Rock 12He who bears ill-will to those who bear ill-will can never become pure; but he who feels no ill-will, pacifies them who hate: as hatred brings misery to mankind, the sage knows no hatred.

Rock 13If a man find a wise companion, who is both steadfast and pure, let him, having overcome all corruption, associate with him, thoughtful and glad.

Rock 14If a man find not a wise companion, who is both steadfast and pure, like a king abandoning his broad kingdom, let him live alone and commit no sin.

Rock 15If a man find not a good friend who leads a life like unto his own, let him resolutely keep a solitary life, and not associate with a fool.

Rock 16It is better to live a lonely life than to have companionship with the fool; casting off all taxation of mind, one lives alone, like the elephant of the Mātanga forest.

Chapter on Hatred, the Fourteenth.
