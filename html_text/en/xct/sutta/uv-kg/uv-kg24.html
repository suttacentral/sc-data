24. Numbers (or Comparisons)

Rock 1It is better to speak one word of sense which brings one nigh unto peace, than to recite a hundred gāthās which are without sense.

Rock 2It is better to speak one word of the law which brings one nigh unto peace, than to recite a hundred gāthās which are not of the law.

Rock 3He who lives a hundred years violating all his vows, a life of one single day is better if one observes all his vows.

Rock 4He who lives a hundred years in laziness and slothfulness, a life of one single day is better if one exerts oneself to zealous application.

Rock 5He who lives a hundred years, his mind without aim or object, a life of one single day is better if one is wise and well composed.

Rock 6He who lives a hundred years without perceiving birth and dissolution, a life of one single day is better if one perceives birth and dissolution.

Rock 7He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the ending of perception Rock 7He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the ending of perception (vedanā), a life of one single day is better if one perceives the ending of perception.

Rock 8He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the end of sin Rock 8He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the end of sin (āsrava), a life of one single day is better if one perceives the end of sin.

Rock 9He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the unchanging place, a life of one single day is better if one perceives the unchanging place Rock 9He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the unchanging place, a life of one single day is better if one perceives the unchanging place (nirvāṇa).

Rock 10He who lives a hundred years without knowing the ideal knowledge that is hard (to arrive at), a life of one single day is better if one knows the ideal knowledge that is hard Rock 10He who lives a hundred years without knowing the ideal knowledge that is hard (to arrive at), a life of one single day is better if one knows the ideal knowledge that is hard (to arrive at).

Rock 11He who lives a hundred years without perceiving what is most exalted, a life of one single day is better if one perceives what is most exalted.

Rock 12He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the perfection of the holy (law), a life of one single day is better if one perceives the perfection of the holy Rock 12He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the perfection of the holy (law), a life of one single day is better if one perceives the perfection of the holy (law, i.e., nirvāṇa).

Rock 13He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the perfect cessation of death, a life of one single day is better if one perceives the perfect cessation of death.

Rock 14He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the most perfect amrita, a life of one single day is better if one perceives the most perfect amrita.

Rock 15He who lives a hundred years without perceiving perfect passionlessness, a life of one single day is better if one perceives perfect passionlessness.

Rock 16He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the perfect absence of passion, a life of one single day is better if one perceives the perfect absence of passion Rock 16He who lives a hundred years without perceiving the perfect absence of passion, a life of one single day is better if one perceives the perfect absence of passion (raga).

Rock 17If a man live for a hundred years in a forest, wholly relying on the fire Rock 17If a man live for a hundred years in a forest, wholly relying on the fire (Agni), and if he but for one single moment pays homage to a man who meditates on the self, this homage is greater than sacrifices for a hundred years.

Rock 18He who month after month eats his food with a tip Rock 18He who month after month eats his food with a tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who has faith in the Buddha.

Rock 19He who month after month eats his food with a tip Rock 19He who month after month eats his food with a tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who has faith in the holy law.

Rock 20He who month after month eats his food with a tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who has faith in the church Rock 20He who month after month eats his food with a tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who has faith in the church (Sangha).

Rock 21He who month after month eats his food with a tip Rock 21He who month after month eats his food with a tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who is merciful to sentient creatures.

Rock 22He who month after month eats his food with a tip Rock 22He who month after month eats his food with a tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who is merciful to animated creatures.

Rock 23He who month after month eats his food with a tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who is merciful to beings Rock 23He who month after month eats his food with a tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who is merciful to beings (bhuta).

Rock 24He who month after month eats his food with the tip Rock 24He who month after month eats his food with the tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who seeks to show kindness.

Rock 25He who month after month eats his food with the tip Rock 25He who month after month eats his food with the tip (of a blade) of kuśa grass, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who explains well the holy law.

Rock 26He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who has faith in the Buddha.

Rock 27He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who has faith in the holy law.

Rock 28He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who has faith in the church Rock 28He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who has faith in the church (Sangha).

Rock 29He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who is merciful to sentient creatures.

Rock 30He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who is merciful to animated creatures.

Rock 31He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who is merciful to beings Rock 31He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who is merciful to beings (bhuta).

Rock 32He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who seeks to show kindness.

Rock 33He who for a hundred years makes a thousand sacrifices each month, is not worth the sixteenth part of him who explains well the holy law.

Rock 34No matter what sacrifice a man may offer in this world to acquire merit, it is not worth the quarter of doing homage to one who has a quieted and upright mind.

Chapter on “Numbers,” the Twenty-fourth.
