18. The Flower

Rock 1Who is there that can overcome the world of the gods, of the lord of death Rock 1Who is there that can overcome the world of the gods, of the lord of death (Yama) and of men, who knows how to expose the most delightful law, as one would flowers?

Rock 2It is the disciple Rock 2It is the disciple (sekhas) who can overcome the world of the gods, of the lord of death and of men, who knows how to expose the most delightful law, as one would flowers.

Rock 3Fear is born of the forest (of ignorance); so cut down not (only) the trees of the forest, but all that appertains to the forest Rock 3Fear is born of the forest (of ignorance); so cut down not (only) the trees of the forest, but all that appertains to the forest (i.e., the roots), and then the Śramana will find nirvāṇa.

Rock 4If man destroys not everything that appertains to the forest down to the smallest part, his mind will be held in bondage, as the calf that wants milk is to its mother' href='#wrh4'>Rock 4If man destroys not everything that appertains to the forest down to the smallest part, his mind will be held in bondage, as the calf that wants milk is to its mother's side.

Rock 5Cut out the love of self as you would an autumn lotus; cherish the road of peace on account of the nirvāṇa which the Victorious One has explained.

Rock 6Like a pretty flower, of pleasing colour but without scent, are the agreeably spoken but fruitless words of one who does not act Rock 6Like a pretty flower, of pleasing colour but without scent, are the agreeably spoken but fruitless words of one who does not act (accordingly).

Rock 7As the bee, which harms neither the colour nor the scent of the flower, but having sucked it flies away, so let the Muni walk through a village.

Rock 8Remarking not the sins of others, and what they have done or left undone, one should remark what he himself does right or wrong.

Rock 9As on piles of filth and in dirty water grows, unaffected by them, the padma, pure, sweet-smelling, and lovely.

Rock 10So the disciple of the perfect Buddha shines by his wisdom among other men, who are blind and Rock 10So the disciple of the perfect Buddha shines by his wisdom among other men, who are blind and (like) a heap of filth.

Rock 11As out of a heap of flowers many garlands are made, so when a man has been born he can do many virtuous deeds.

Rock 12As the vakula plant in summer sheds all its flowers, so let the Bhixu drop passions, hatred, and ignorance.

Rock 13A man whose mind is troubled, like one gathering flowers, the lord of death carries him off as the flood does a sleeping village.

Rock 14A man whose mind is troubled, like one gathering flowers, falls into the power of the lord of death without having satisfied his desires.

Rock 15A man whose mind is troubled, like one gathering: flowers, falls into the power of the lord of death without him having acquired wealth Rock 15A man whose mind is troubled, like one gathering: flowers, falls into the power of the lord of death without him having acquired wealth (enough to satisfy him).

Rock 16A man whose mind is troubled, like one gathering flowers, falls into the power of the lord of death without having arrived at the object of his pursuit.

Rock 17He who has perceived that this body is (empty) as a vase, and who knows that all things (dharma) are as an illusion, does thus destroy the chief of Māra' href='#wrh17'>Rock 17He who has perceived that this body is (empty) as a vase, and who knows that all things (dharma) are as an illusion, does thus destroy the chief of Māra's flowers, and will no more be seen by the king of death.

Rock 18He who has perceived that this world is like froth, and who knows that all things are as an illusion, does thus destroy the chief of Māra' href='#wrh18'>Rock 18He who has perceived that this world is like froth, and who knows that all things are as an illusion, does thus destroy the chief of Māra's flowers, and will no more be seen by the king of death.

Rock 19He who has perceived that this body is like froth, and who knows that all things are as an illusion, does thus destroy the chief of Māra' href='#wrh19'>Rock 19He who has perceived that this body is like froth, and who knows that all things are as an illusion, does thus destroy the chief of Māra's flowers, and will no more be seen by the king of death.

Rock 20Rock 20Repetition of verse 18.

Rock 21The Bhixu who knows that existence is without reality, like an udumbāra flower, casts off what is and is not of the other shore, as a snake shuffles off his old dried-up skin.

Rock 22The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of passion, as one does the flower from the water-born Rock 22The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of passion, as one does the flower from the water-born (lotus) growing in a tank, casts off what is and is not of the other shore, as a snake shuffles off his old dried-up skin.

Rock 23The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of hatred, as one does the flower from the water-born Rock 23The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of hatred, as one does the flower from the water-born (lotus) growing in a tank, casts off what is and is not of the other shore, as a snake shuffles off his old dried-up skin.

Rock 24The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of ignorance, as one does the flower of the water-born Rock 24The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of ignorance, as one does the flower of the water-born (lotus) growing in a tank, casts off what is and is not of the other shore, as a snake shuffles off his old dried-up skin.

Rock 25The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of egotism, as one does the flower of the water-born Rock 25The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of egotism, as one does the flower of the water-born (lotus) growing in a tank, casts off what is and is not of the other shore, as a snake shuffles off his old dried-up skin.

Rock 26The Bhixu who cuts of every particle of affection, as one does the flower of the water-born Rock 26The Bhixu who cuts of every particle of affection, as one does the flower of the water-born (lotus) growing in a tank, casts off what is and is not of the other shore, as a snake shuffles off his old dried-up skin.

Rock 27The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of desire, as one does the flower of the water-born Rock 27The Bhixu who cuts off every particle of desire, as one does the flower of the water-born (lotus) growing in a tank, casts off what is and is not of the other shore, as a snake shuffles off his old dried-up skin.
